Do I have to participate in all parts of Springboard to Success?

Yes. All sessions are designed to give you the basics to start your education here at SIUE. You will also meet with an academic advisor and schedule your first semester of courses.

What do parents/guests do at Springboard to Success?

Here is a link to the 2016 Parent Agenda. Note that for the 1-Day Springboard to Success sessions, there is a separate agenda: 2016 1-Day Agenda.

What should I bring to Springboard to Success?

You will need to bring your student ID number (found in your admission packet), photo identification (such as a driver's license or state issued identification card), any personal items required for an overnight stay (we provide linens and towels, but other than this, if you need it to start or end your day, please bring it with you), comfortable clothing and shoes (you will be walking around campus), and a sweatshirt or jacket.

Will I get my student ID at Springboard to Success?

Yes. Students will have the opportunity to get their CougarCard at Springboard to Success. Be sure to bring a valid state photo identification (such as a driver's license). High School student ID cards will not be accepted.

Can I turn in my health immunization forms at Springboard to Success?

Yes. Please bring in your completed form you received in your admission packet. If you need one, you can download a new form.

Is there a charge for Springboard to Success?

There is a non-refundable enrollment and registration fee for Springboard to Success.

Can I come to any program I'd like?

Springboard to Success session dates are limited to 200 students per session and are filled as space is available. You may select any date that best fits your summer schedule, as long as the date is still available. Please note: once you have selected a session date, it is difficult to change.

Can parents/guests sit in on my Academic Advising session?

Yes. Parents/guests are more than welcome to be a part of their students' academic advising. These sessions will be in group settings, but allow for individual time as well. Parents/guests will not be allowed to accompany their students during course registration. Unfortunately, the computer labs where the students will be registering are not conducive to parent/guest attendance.

Can my parents/guests stay overnight in the residence halls?

No. Parents/guests are responsible for making their own off-campus accomodations. Here is a website with directions and a list of local accomodations.

Where can I park on campus?

Students and parents/guests may park in Visitor Parking Lot B. There will be Springboard to Success Student Leaders in the parking lot from 11:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. If you enter Visitor Lot B before a Springboard Student Leader arrives at 11:30 a.m., you will need to pay at one of the Kiosks for parking. If you enter Visitor Lot B, you will need to pick up a two-day parking pass from the registration table in Morris University Center's Meridian Ballroom.

Can I bring a friend who will not be attending SIUE as a new freshmen?

No. Only SIUE admitted freshmen and their family members may attend Springboard to Success. A friend who would like to visit the campus is always welcome to contact the Office of Admissions at (800) 447-SIUE to set up a tour.

Will I have to take placement tests?

You must complete all placement tests before attending Springboard to Success. To determine whether you need to take placement tests, contact the Office of Admissions at (800) 447-SIUE or (618) 650-3705, or springboard@siue.edu.

How do I determine whether or not I am missing Financial Aid documents?

Steps for checking missing documents:

  1. Go to Cougarnet
  2. Click "Enter CougarNet with your e-ID" (1st option) or "Enter CougarNet with you University ID" (2nd option)
  3. Click "Financial Aid" (3rd tab)
  4. Click "Eligibility" (2nd option)
  5. Click "Student Requirements" (1st tab)
  6. Current Academic Year Requirements will appear
  7. "Established" means the document must immediately be turned into Financial Aid
